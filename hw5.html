  I am going to list some of the more common plants that you would see in San Francisco.
  You will find that many of the plants that you see around the city are actually not
  native to the Bay Area, yet are associated as though they are.

  The Tasmanian blue gum, Eucalyptus globulus, is an impressive tree.
  Tall, twisted, multi colored bark, with white flowers like sea anemones, blue gum
  eucalyptus are associated with the San Francisco Bay area,
  irregardless of it being native to Australlia.
  They love the climate, and many were planted either for timber or
  for scenery from the 1850s onwards.

  Ice Plant, also known as Hottentot Fig, was originally used along highways to stabilize
  blowing dunes, and is native to South Africa. The plant is everywhere along the coast.
  They are a low lying shruby plant with bright pink, white, and yellow flowers.

  Sea Cliff Buckwheat is one of my favorite native plants in the Bay.
  It has an overall silvery appearance with with fluffy pink to white flower clusters
  that sit atop thin tall stocks. You can see them in places likes Lands End and Fort Funston.

  Coastal Redwoods are the giants of our coasts. These incredible trees are native to
  the west coast and the furry red bark and finely needled green leaves make them standout.
  You can see them throughout Golden Gate Park.
